# Solana (SOL)

## Identity
Solana is a high-performance, single-layer (monolithic) blockchain launched in March 2020, created by Anatoly Yakovenko and the Solana Labs team and supported by the Solana Foundation. Its native asset is SOL. Solana targets very high throughput and low fees by optimizing the base layer rather than relying on layer-2 rollups, and it hosts DeFi, NFTs, payments, and consumer applications.

## Consensus
Solana combines Proof of History (PoH) with Proof of Stake (PoS). Proof of History is a verifiable delay function that timestamps and orders events before consensus, letting validators agree on time without extensive communication. On top of PoH, a PoS-based Tower BFT mechanism selects leaders and finalizes blocks. Validators and delegators stake SOL; voting power is proportional to stake. This design enables sub-second block times and high transaction throughput.

## Supply schedule
Solana does not have a fixed maximum supply. It launched with an initial inflation schedule that began around 8% annualized and decreases by roughly 15% per year until reaching a long-term tail inflation of about 1.5%. Staking rewards are funded by this inflation. A portion of each transaction fee is burned, which partially offsets issuance. Circulating supply also reflects vesting from early allocations.

## Use case
Solana is designed for applications that need high speed and low cost: decentralized exchanges, order-book and automated-market-maker trading, NFT marketplaces, payments, and high-frequency consumer apps. Programs (smart contracts) are commonly written in Rust. Its parallel transaction execution engine (Sealevel) processes non-overlapping transactions simultaneously.

## Key risks
Solana has experienced network outages and performance degradations under heavy load, which is a notable reliability concern. Other risks include validator hardware requirements that can favor well-resourced operators (a centralization concern), historical stake concentration, smart-contract risk in applications, and competition from other high-throughput chains and layer-2 networks.

## On-chain footprint
Solana uses an account-based model with parallel execution. Commonly tracked metrics include transactions per second, total daily transactions (including vote transactions), active addresses, total value locked in DeFi, and the share and number of staked SOL. Validator count and stake distribution are watched as decentralization indicators.
