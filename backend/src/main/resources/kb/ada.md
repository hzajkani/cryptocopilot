# Cardano (ADA)

## Identity
Cardano is a decentralized smart-contract platform launched in 2017, co-founded by Ethereum co-founder Charles Hoskinson and developed with input from IOG (formerly IOHK), the Cardano Foundation, and Emurgo. Its native asset is ADA, named after Ada Lovelace. Cardano is known for a research-driven approach, with protocol designs published as peer-reviewed academic papers before implementation, and development organized into named eras (Byron, Shelley, Goguen, Basho, Voltaire).

## Consensus
Cardano uses Ouroboros, a Proof-of-Stake protocol with formal security proofs. Time is divided into epochs and slots; stake pools are elected to produce blocks with probability proportional to the stake delegated to them. ADA holders can delegate their stake to pools without locking or risking their funds, and delegators share in rewards. Ouroboros has several variants (Classic, BFT, Praos, Genesis) introduced over time.

## Supply schedule
Cardano has a fixed maximum supply of 45 billion ADA. New ADA enters circulation from a decreasing reserve that funds staking rewards each epoch, alongside transaction fees that are recycled into rewards and a treasury. Because issuance comes from a capped reserve that depletes over time, the rate of new supply declines, and total supply cannot exceed 45 billion.

## Use case
ADA is used to pay transaction fees, to stake/delegate for securing the network and earning rewards, and for on-chain governance. Cardano supports smart contracts via the Plutus platform (using a UTXO-extended model called EUTXO) and Marlowe for financial contracts. Target applications include DeFi, identity, and use cases in emerging markets.

## Key risks
Risks include a deliberate, methodical development pace that some perceive as slow relative to competitors; comparatively lower DeFi and application adoption; the learning curve of the EUTXO model and functional programming; competition among smart-contract platforms; and general regulatory uncertainty for tokens and staking.

## On-chain footprint
Cardano uses an extended UTXO (EUTXO) accounting model. Tracked metrics include daily transactions, active addresses, the amount of ADA staked and the number of active stake pools, delegation distribution, and treasury balances. Stake participation is typically high, reflecting the non-custodial delegation design.
