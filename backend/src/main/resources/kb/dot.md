# Polkadot (DOT)

## Identity
Polkadot is a decentralized, multi-chain network launched in May 2020, founded by Ethereum co-founder Gavin Wood and developed with the Web3 Foundation and Parity Technologies. Its native asset is DOT. Polkadot is designed for interoperability and shared security, allowing many specialized blockchains to connect and communicate. Gavin Wood also authored the Substrate framework used to build Polkadot-compatible chains.

## Consensus
Polkadot uses Nominated Proof of Stake (NPoS). Validators secure the network and are backed by nominators who bond DOT to back trustworthy validators and share rewards and slashing risk. Block production and finality are split into two sub-protocols: BABE assigns block-production slots, while GRANDPA provides finality by finalizing chains of blocks. The central relay chain provides shared security to the connected chains.

## Supply schedule
Polkadot does not have a fixed maximum supply. DOT issuance is inflationary, with a target staking rate that adjusts rewards; annual inflation has been on the order of about 10%, split between validators/nominators and the on-chain treasury. Unclaimed inflation is directed to the treasury. (Polkadot originally redenominated DOT by a factor of 100 in 2020.)

## Use case
DOT is used for staking to secure the network, for on-chain governance, and for "bonding" to acquire parachain slots. Parachains are application-specific blockchains that connect to the relay chain and share its security; they communicate through Cross-Consensus Messaging (XCM). Use cases span DeFi, identity, and specialized enterprise or community chains built with Substrate.

## Key risks
Risks include the complexity of the parachain model and slot-allocation economics; competition from other interoperability and layer-1/layer-2 solutions; governance complexity; validator and nominator concentration; and general regulatory uncertainty. The cost and mechanics of securing parachain slots can be a barrier for projects.

## On-chain footprint
The relay chain coordinates validators, nominators, and parachains. Tracked metrics include the amount of DOT staked and the staking ratio, validator and nominator counts, the number of active parachains, treasury balance and spending, and cross-chain (XCM) message activity.
