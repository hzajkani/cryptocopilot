# BNB (BNB)

## Identity
BNB (formerly "Binance Coin") is the native asset of the BNB Chain ecosystem, originally issued by the Binance exchange in 2017 via an ICO as an ERC-20 token and later migrated to its own chain. BNB Chain today comprises the BNB Smart Chain (BSC), an EVM-compatible chain for smart contracts, alongside the older BNB Beacon Chain (which has been deprecated/sunset). BNB is used for fees, utility, and governance within this ecosystem.

## Consensus
BNB Smart Chain uses Proof of Staked Authority (PoSA), a hybrid of Proof of Authority and Delegated Proof of Stake. A limited set of validators (historically on the order of 21, later expanded to around 41 active validators) is selected based on staked BNB to produce blocks in rounds. This yields fast block times and low fees, at the cost of a smaller, more permissioned validator set than fully open PoW or PoS networks.

## Supply schedule
BNB launched with a total supply of 200 million tokens. The supply is reduced over time through token burns, with the stated goal of reaching 100 million BNB. Burns include a periodic auto-burn mechanism (which adjusts the burn amount based on price and the number of blocks produced) and real-time gas-fee burning on BNB Smart Chain. As a result, BNB is a disinflationary asset whose circulating supply decreases as burns proceed.

## Use case
BNB is used to pay trading-fee discounts on the Binance exchange, to pay gas fees on BNB Smart Chain, for participation in token sales, and across DeFi, payments, and applications in the ecosystem. It also functions as the staking and governance asset for the chain's validators.

## Key risks
Key risks include centralization of the validator set and close association with a single company (Binance), which concentrates governance and regulatory exposure; regulatory actions against Binance in multiple jurisdictions; smart-contract and bridge exploits seen historically on BSC; and the discretionary nature of supply burns. Concentration of token holdings is also discussed.

## On-chain footprint
BNB Smart Chain is account-based and EVM-compatible. Tracked metrics include daily transactions, active addresses, gas fees burned, total value locked in BSC DeFi, and validator participation. High transaction volumes reflect the chain's low-fee design.
