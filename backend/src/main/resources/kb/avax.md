# Avalanche (AVAX)

## Identity
Avalanche is a decentralized smart-contract platform launched in September 2020 by Ava Labs, founded by Emin Gün Sirer and colleagues. Its native asset is AVAX. Avalanche emphasizes fast finality and the ability to launch custom, application-specific blockchains called Subnets. Its primary contract chain is EVM-compatible, making it familiar to Ethereum developers.

## Consensus
Avalanche introduced a family of consensus protocols (collectively the Avalanche/Snow family) based on repeated randomized sub-sampling of validators. Each validator repeatedly queries small random samples of other validators and adopts the majority preference; this converges rapidly to network-wide agreement with sub-second finality. It is a Proof-of-Stake system: validators must stake AVAX, and there are no slashing penalties beyond loss of rewards for poor uptime. The Snowman variant provides a linear-chain protocol used for the contract chain.

## Supply schedule
AVAX has a fixed maximum (hard) supply of 720 million tokens. New AVAX is minted as staking rewards, while all transaction fees on the primary network are burned. Because fees are burned and total minting cannot exceed the cap, AVAX has a capped, partly fee-deflationary supply model; the exact circulating amount reflects vesting schedules and staking.

## Use case
Avalanche has a three-chain architecture on its primary network: the Exchange Chain (X-Chain) for assets, the Platform Chain (P-Chain) for staking and coordinating validators and Subnets, and the Contract Chain (C-Chain) for EVM smart contracts. Subnets let projects deploy custom blockchains with their own rules and validator sets, used for DeFi, gaming, institutional, and enterprise applications.

## Key risks
Risks include strong competition among layer-1 platforms and Ethereum layer-2 rollups; the complexity of the multi-chain and Subnet architecture; economic bootstrapping and security of individual Subnets; validator stake concentration; and smart-contract risk in applications. General token and staking regulatory uncertainty also applies.

## On-chain footprint
The C-Chain is account-based and EVM-compatible. Tracked metrics include daily transactions, active addresses, AVAX burned in fees, the amount of AVAX staked and validator/delegator counts, total value locked in DeFi, and the number and activity of Subnets.
